Producing biogas efficiently

The world's fuel supply is threatened by the imminent shortage of petroleum. Humanity may effectively fight this menace with the aid of biogenic methane, but the world is hindered from fully utilizing this technology because its practitioners overlook a key scientific premise: that the output of work is dependent on the energy available for executing that task.
Methane generation is currently inefficient due to the use of low-calorie inputs such as cattle dung, distillery effluent, municipal solid waste, and sewage. The efficiency of methane synthesis may be boosted by many orders of magnitude by employing feedstock with high calorific and nutritional value.
